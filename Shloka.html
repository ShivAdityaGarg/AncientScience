1. ऋग्वेद - Universal Wisdom

आ नो भद्राः क्रतवो यन्तु विश्वतः।

Meaning: Let noble thoughts come to us from every direction in the universe.

Source: Rigveda 1.89.1

2. यशोधरः – Scientific Methodology

स्वहस्तेन कृतं सम्यक् ज्ञारं न श्रुतं मया।
स्वहस्तेन भवद्योगं कृतं सम्यक् श्रुतं हि॥
धातुवस्तुसंग्रहस्ती स्वहस्तेन कृतं मया॥

Meaning: Only that which I verified by experimentation or learnt from experts is included. That which could not be confirmed has been excluded. Students must verify all facts with their own hands—only then are they true seekers.

Source: Commentary by Yashodhara, 13th century CE

3. यंत्रर्णवः – Definition of a Machine

दण्डैश्चक्रैश्च दन्तैश्च सरणिभ्रमणादिभिः।
शक्तेरुत्पादनं किं वा चालनं यन्त्रमुच्यते॥

Meaning: A machine is that which generates power or motion through levers, pulleys, gears, screws, or similar continuous mechanical movement.

Source: Yantrarnavah, 14th century CE

4. वैदिक वैज्ञानिकाः – Ancient Indian Scientists

वैज्ञानिकाश्च कपिलः कणादः सुस्रुतस्तथा।
चरको भास्कराचार्यः वराहमिहिरः सुधीः॥
नागार्जुनो भरद्वाजः आर्यभट्टो वसुर्बुधः।
ध्येयो वैकटरामश्च विष्णु रामानुजादयः॥

Meaning: Scientists such as Kapila, Kanada, Sushruta, Charaka, Bhaskaracharya, Varāhamihira, Nāgārjuna, Bharadvāja, Aryabhata, and others like Vākāṭarāma, Vishnu, and Rāmānuja should be revered(deeply respected or admired).

Source: Sanskrit commemorative verse (popularly recited to honor Indian scientific legacy)

पाणिनिः / Pāṇini ~ वैज्ञानिकवाक्संरचना / Science of Linguistic Structure

वृत्तिः अर्थे, प्रयोजनं च सूचनम्।
— अष्टाध्यायी १.१.१

"A rule must convey meaning and serve a purpose." — Aṣṭādhyāyī 1.1.1

This foundational sutra by Pāṇini reflects a deeply scientific outlook. His treatise "Aṣṭādhyāyī" is not just a grammar manual, but a rule-based, algorithmic system that mirrors the logic of modern computing. His method of linguistic analysis, using finite rules, meta-rules, and systematic prioritization, laid the groundwork for the structure of formal languages and syntax used in artificial intelligence and programming today.

Source: Aṣṭādhyāyī 1.1.1 by Pāṇini (circa 4th century BCE)
